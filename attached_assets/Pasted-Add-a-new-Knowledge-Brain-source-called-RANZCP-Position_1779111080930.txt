Add a new Knowledge Brain source called “RANZCP Position Statements”.

Purpose:
The app should learn from all official RANZCP position statements and use them to improve Quiz Mode, Daily MEQs, Exam Mode, signal maps, examiner feedback, and consultant-level reasoning.

Important:
Use only official RANZCP sources.
Do not use unofficial summaries.
Do not copy long passages verbatim into generated questions or feedback.
Use position statements to understand principles, policy positions, ethical framing, service standards, cultural safety, psychiatrist role, governance, advocacy and systems reasoning.

Create a new admin page:
“RANZCP Position Statements Brain”

This page should allow:

1. Add official RANZCP position statement
Fields:
- Title
- RANZCP reference number, e.g. PS #105
- Topic tags
- Last updated date
- Published jurisdiction
- Source URL
- Status: current / rescinded / archived / unknown
- Summary
- Key principles
- MEQ relevance
- Consultant-level implications
- Related signal categories

2. Bulk upload/import
Allow me to upload PDFs, DOCX, TXT or CSV files containing RANZCP position statements or summaries.

Allow me to paste official RANZCP position statement links.

If extraction is not available, create placeholder cards that I can fill manually.

- last updated date

7. Integration with Quiz Mode

When generating quiz stems, the app should use relevant RANZCP position statements to create more consultant-level stems.

Examples:
- Cultural safety statement should influence Aboriginal and Māori mental health stems.
- Trauma-informed practice should influence ED, inpatient, restraint, refugee, family violence and child/adolescent stems.
- Suicide prevention should influence risk, discharge, systems and family/carer questions.
- Rural psychiatry should influence regional/resource-limited cases.
- Role of psychiatrist statement should influence MDT leadership and consultant responsibility questions.
- Mental health systems principles should influence governance and service pressure questions.

8. Integration with marking

When assessing the candidate’s answer, the AI should check whether the candidate identified position-statement-level issues where relevant.

Examples:
- Did the candidate recognise cultural safety rather than only “use interpreter”?
- Did the candidate avoid retraumatisation?
- Did the candidate include lived-experience or family/carer partnership where relevant?
- Did the candidate show systems leadership, not just individual clinical management?
- Did the candidate identify rural access/resource equity issues?
- Did the candidate understand psychiatrist role in MDT conflict and governance?

9. Strict feedback rule

The app should not give generic feedback like:
“Consider cultural factors.”

Instead it should say:
“The stem specifically gave Aboriginal/Māori/culturally relevant details. You should have identified cultural safety as a clinical and systems issue, not merely a communication preference. This affects engagement, coercion, family involvement, liaison, least restrictive care and risk management.”

10. Citation/source display

In feedback, show a small expandable section:
“Relevant RANZCP principle used”

This should include:
- position statement title
- reference number
- last updated date
- short principle summary
- source link

Do not overload the candidate during timed answering.
Show this only after submission.
