## FINAL-081

Bed pressure is real, but it must not decide the care of a patient with acute-on-chronic suicide risk, trauma, BPD dynamics and a split ward team. In chronic suicidality, denial of suicidal thoughts is neither necessary nor sufficient for safe discharge. The ethical tension is between duty of care and avoiding iatrogenic admission. I would document the acute-on-chronic formulation, the leave refusal trigger, the threat quote, bed pressure, family anxiety, team splitting, risk assessment, discharge/admission rationale and specific follow-up plan. A defensible note shows that the consultant did not discharge because ED needed the bed, and did not admit indefinitely because of a coercive threat, but made a structured therapeutic decision.
