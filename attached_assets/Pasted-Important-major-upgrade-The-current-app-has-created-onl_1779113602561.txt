QUIZ MODE FLOW

Before submission:
- Show only the stem.
- Show the timer.
- Show a blank free-text answer box.
- Do not show expected signals.
- Do not show checklist.
- Do not show hints.
- Do not show categories.

Timer:
- Add 2-minute and 3-minute options.
- Candidate must press Start.
- Add Stop/Pause.
- Add Reset.
- When time is over, show “Time finished” but allow submission.

After submission:
The app must analyse the candidate’s free-text answer against the expected signal map.

Strict examiner feedback should show:

1. Score
- X / Y

2. Overall judgement
- Strong / borderline / weak

3. Correctly identified signals
For each:
- candidate wording
- matched expected signal
- exact clue in the stem
- why this mattered

4. Critical missed signals
For each:
- missed signal
- exact clue in the stem
- why the candidate should have identified it
- risk/legal/cultural/system implication
- how it should have been written

5. Unsupported or overcalled signals
For each:
- candidate claim
- why it was not supported by the stem
- corrected reasoning

6. Priority ranking
- most urgent signals
- secondary signals
- lower-yield signals

7. Final corrected signal list
Group by:
- immediate safety
- diagnosis/formulation
- risk to self
- risk to others
- risk to child/unborn/vulnerable person
- substance use
- physical health
- capacity
- Mental Health Act
- consent/refusal
- cultural safety
- trauma-informed care
- family/carer conflict
- child protection
- collateral
- MDT conflict
- ED/inpatient/system pressure
- quality and governance
- supervision/documentation
- disposition planning

8. Consultant-level correction
Write exactly what the candidate should have written in concise MEQ style.

STRICT MARKING STYLE

The examiner must not be suggestive.
The examiner must not give generic psychiatry teaching.
The examiner must not say “consider everything”.
The examiner must only mark what is supported by the stem.
For every missed signal, it must say exactly why that signal was present in the question.
The feedback must say:
“This was given in the stem because…”
“This should have triggered…”
“The clinical/legal/system implication is…”
