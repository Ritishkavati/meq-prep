Core idea:
Quiz Mode is not an answer-writing MEQ. It is a “signal detection” mode.
The app gives an MEQ-style stem. Candidate has 2–3 minutes to identify all the hidden issues/signals in the stem.
After submission, the app marks strictly like a consultant examiner.

Very important:
Before submission, do NOT show the expected signals or checklist.
The candidate should not be guided or prompted.
Only show the question stem and a blank answer box.
Expected signals must appear only after the candidate submits.

Quiz Mode flow:

1. Quiz setup screen
Show:
- Topic selector: Random / ED / Perinatal / CL Psychiatry / Forensic / Old Age / Child & Adolescent / Governance / Cultural Safety / Substance Use / Risk / Capacity & Mental Health Act
- Difficulty selector: Standard / Difficult / Consultant-level
- Time selector: 2 minutes / 3 minutes
- Button: “Generate Quiz Stem”

2. Generate Quiz Stem
The app should generate an original MEQ-style stem using:
- uploaded old MEQ questions
- uploaded marking guides
- uploaded preparation material

- contemporary psychiatry knowledge
- Australian/NZ consultant-level practice reasoning

Copyright rule:
Do not copy old uploaded exam questions verbatim.
Use uploaded material to understand patterns, complexity, themes, stem structure, examiner focus and signal types.
Generate original fictional stems that feel similar in structure and complexity to real MEQ-style questions.

3. Quiz screen
Show:
- MEQ-style stem
- setting
- candidate role
- complexity cues
- stakeholder conflict if relevant
- systems/resource issue if relevant
- timer box
- Start button
- Stop button
- Reset button
- Submit answer button
- Text area: “List all signals/issues you identify from this stem”

Timer:
- Candidate gets maximum 2–3 minutes depending on selected setting.
- Timer should not start automatically.
- Candidate presses Start.
- Stop pauses the timer.
- Submit is allowed after start.
- When time reaches zero, show “Time finished” but allow submission.
- Record time used.

4. Candidate answer
Candidate writes free text only.
Do not use checkbox selection before submission.
Do not show signal hints.
Do not show categories before submission.
The candidate must independently identify the signals.

5. AI analysis after submission
After submission, send to AI:
- generated stem
- candidate answer
- expected signal map
- topic
- difficulty
- time used
- uploaded knowledge brain context

AI must act as a strict consultant psychiatrist examiner, not a tutor.

Feedback must be structured like this:

Score:
- X / Y

Overall judgement:
- Strong / borderline / weak

Signals correctly identified:
For each:
- Candidate wording
- Correct signal
- Why this was present in the stem
- Why it matters clinically

Critical missed signals:
For each:
- Missed signal
- Exact clue from the stem
- Why the candidate should have identified it
- What risk/legal/cultural/system implication it carries

Incorrect or overcalled signals:
For each:
- Candidate claim
- Why it is not supported by the stem
- How to correct the reasoning

Examiner correction:
- What the candidate should have written
- Use concise consultant-level wording

Priority ranking:
- Which signals were most urgent
- Which signals were secondary
- Which signals were low-yield

Final corrected signal list:
- Immediate safety signals
- Diagnostic/formulation signals
- Risk signals
- Capacity/legal signals
- Cultural safety signals
- Family/carer signals
- MDT/system/governance signals
- Treatment/disposition signals

Strict examiner rules:
- Do not give generic psychiatry teaching.
- Do not say “consider everything” unless the stem supports it.
- For every missed point, explain the exact phrase or fact in the stem that should have triggered it.
- Mark based on what is written, not what the candidate probably meant.
- Penalise vague wording.
- Penalise generic lists.
- Penalise failure to use vignette details.
- Reward specific linkage: “This clue means this risk/legal/system issue.”
- Be direct and consultant-level.
- Do not over-reassure.
